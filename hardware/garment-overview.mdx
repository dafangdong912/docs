The PressureLight 压感光护 is a smart safety garment engineered to detect when you're in distress and respond instantly — flashing high-visibility alerts, triggering tactile alarms, and enabling an SOS call from your iPhone, all without requiring a single app install. Understanding the hardware inside the garment helps you wear it correctly, maintain it, and trust it when it matters most.

## Components Overview

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Pressure Sensor Matrix" icon="grid-2">
    A grid of pressure-sensitive sensors woven directly into the garment fabric. The matrix continuously monitors pressure patterns across the sensor zones and feeds that data to the ESP32. When it detects sustained distress-level pressure — distinct from normal movement — it signals the ESP32 to activate the SOS sequence.
  </Card>
  <Card title="ESP32 Controller" icon="microchip">
    The brain of the PressureLight system. The ESP32 microcontroller reads input from the pressure matrix, applies threshold logic to distinguish distress from everyday activity, and takes action — controlling the LED light strip, firing the vibration motor, and managing overall SOS state. Everything runs on-device with no cloud dependency.
  </Card>
  <Card title="LED Light Strip" icon="lightbulb">
    High-visibility LED strips run along the exterior of the garment. On SOS activation, the ESP32 drives the strips into a rapid alternating red-and-white flash pattern (红白闪烁) that is designed to be seen by bystanders and emergency services from a distance, day or night.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Vibration Motor" icon="wave-pulse">
    An embedded vibration motor activates simultaneously with the LED strip during SOS. It delivers a strong tactile alert directly to you — the wearer — while also producing enough physical sensation and sound to attract the attention of anyone nearby.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Before You Go Out

Run through this checklist every time before you head out wearing your PressureLight:

- Garment is fully charged
- LED strips are unobstructed by outerwear or accessories
- Pressure sensors are in contact with your body — the garment fits correctly and snugly
- The SOS helper page is bookmarked in Safari on your iPhone
- Your emergency contact number is known and accessible

## Charging the Garment

Charge your PressureLight using the included cable before each use. Connect the cable to the charging port on the garment and plug the other end into a power source. The onboard LED indicator shows charge status:

- **Solid red** — charging in progress
- **Solid green** — fully charged
- **Flashing red** — battery critically low; charge immediately

Allow the garment to reach a full charge before wearing it for extended outdoor activity. Do not use the garment while it is charging.

<Note>
  **Care and Maintenance** — Avoid fully submerging the garment in water; it is not rated for immersion. Follow the washing instructions on the garment label carefully. Do not fold, crease, or sharply bend the LED strip sections — doing so can damage the strip and impair the visual alert.
</Note>
