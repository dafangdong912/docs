PressureLight turns a single physical trigger into a coordinated chain of visual alarms, tactile feedback, and a precise GPS-powered emergency message. This page walks you through every stage of that chain so you know exactly what to expect when seconds matter.

## The SOS Flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pressure Matrix Detects Distress">
    The fabric-embedded pressure sensor matrix continuously monitors for sustained pressure at designated trigger zones on the garment. When the sensors detect a deliberate, sustained press that matches the SOS threshold, they send a signal to the onboard ESP32 microcontroller. The ESP32 immediately begins executing the alert sequence — no buttons, no unlocking, no manual steps required from you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Garment Alerts Begin">
    The ESP32 activates both the LED strips and the vibration motor simultaneously. The LED strips flash alternating red and white light in a rapid, high-visibility pattern designed to attract attention from bystanders at a distance. At the same time, the vibration motor pulses against your body, giving you clear tactile confirmation that the SOS sequence is running — even if you cannot see or hear the lights around you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the SOS Helper Page">
    You or a nearby bystander opens the PressureLight mobile SOS helper page in **iPhone Safari**. The URL is printed on the garment's care label and encoded in a QR code on the garment tag. The page loads instantly — no app download, no account login. Once the page is open, enter the wearer's name and the emergency contact's phone number if they are not already pre-filled.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Capture GPS and Notify">
    Tap **获取我的当前位置 (Get Location)**. Safari prompts for location permission; grant it to allow the page to read the iPhone's GPS. The page displays the captured latitude, longitude, and accuracy in meters. Then tap either **复制求助信息** to copy the full SOS message to the clipboard, or **打开短信发送** to open a pre-filled SMS deep-link addressed to your emergency contact. Confirm and send the message — your contact receives the full alert with dual map links immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example SOS Message

Every message sent through PressureLight follows this exact format, giving your emergency contact everything they need to locate and assist you:

```plaintext
【PressureLight 紧急求助】
<Name> 触发了智能压力感知服装 SOS 报警。
当前位置：纬度 <lat>，经度 <lng>
定位精度：约 <X> 米
时间：<timestamp>
Apple 地图：https://maps.apple.com/?ll=<lat>,<lng>&q=PressureLight%20SOS
Google 地图：https://maps.google.com/?q=<lat>,<lng>
请尽快联系或前往协助。
```

The `<Name>` field is populated from the wearer name you enter on the helper page. The `<lat>`, `<lng>`, `<X>`, and `<timestamp>` values are filled in automatically from the iPhone's GPS reading at the moment you tap 'Get Location'.

<Info>
  The SOS helper page **cannot silently access your location**. iPhone Safari enforces a mandatory user-permission prompt before any GPS data is read. You must tap 'Get Location' and grant location access for coordinates to appear. This is a deliberate privacy protection — your location is never captured in the background or without your action.
</Info>
