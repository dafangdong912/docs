PressureLight turns a single physical trigger into a coordinated chain of visual alarms, tactile feedback, and a precise GPS-powered emergency message. This page walks you through every stage of that chain so you know exactly what to expect when seconds matter.

## The SOS Flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pressure Matrix Detects Distress">
    The fabric-embedded pressure sensor matrix continuously monitors for sustained pressure at designated trigger zones on the garment. When the sensors detect a deliberate, sustained press that matches the SOS threshold, they send a signal to the onboard ESP32 microcontroller. The ESP32 immediately begins executing the alert sequence — no buttons, no unlocking, no manual steps required from you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Garment Alerts Begin">
    The ESP32 activates both the LED strips and the vibration motor simultaneously. The LED strips flash alternating red and white light in a rapid, high-visibility pattern designed to attract attention from bystanders at a distance. At the same time, the vibration motor pulses against your body, giving you clear tactile confirmation that the SOS sequence is running — even if you cannot see or hear the lights around you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the SOS Helper Page">
    You or a nearby bystander opens the PressureLight mobile SOS helper page in **iPhone Safari**. The URL is printed on the garment's care label and encoded in a QR code on the garment tag. The page loads instantly — no app download, no account login. Once the page is open, enter the wearer's name and the emergency contact's phone number if they are not already pre-filled.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Capture GPS Location">
    Tap **获取我的当前位置 (Get Location)**. Safari prompts for location permission; grant it to allow the page to read the iPhone's GPS. The page uses the browser's `navigator.geolocation.getCurrentPosition` API with `enableHighAccuracy: true`, a 15-second timeout, and `maximumAge: 0` to always return a fresh, high-accuracy reading. Once the position is acquired, the page displays your latitude and longitude (to six decimal places) and the accuracy of the reading in meters.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the SOS Message">
    With coordinates displayed, tap either **复制求助信息** to copy the full SOS message to your clipboard, or **打开短信发送** to open a pre-filled SMS deep-link addressed to your emergency contact. Confirm and send the message in the Messages app — your contact receives the full alert with dual map links immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## GPS Capture Details

The SOS helper page requests your location using the following parameters, giving you the most accurate position the iPhone hardware can provide:

| Parameter | Value | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| `enableHighAccuracy` | `true` | Forces the iPhone to use GPS hardware rather than Wi-Fi or cell triangulation |
| `timeout` | `15000 ms` | Waits up to 15 seconds before reporting a timeout error |
| `maximumAge` | `0` | Always requests a fresh position — never uses a cached location |

### GPS Error Codes

If the location request fails, the helper page displays one of the following errors:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Error 1 — Permission Denied">
    You denied location access when Safari prompted, or location services are disabled for Safari in iPhone Settings. To fix this, go to **Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services → Safari** and set it to **While Using the App**, then reload the helper page and tap 'Get Location' again.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Error 2 — Position Unavailable">
    The iPhone cannot determine your location — this typically happens indoors with no GPS signal and weak Wi-Fi. Move to a window or step outside and try again.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Error 3 — Timeout">
    The GPS hardware did not return a position within 15 seconds. This usually means you are in a location with poor satellite visibility. Move to an open outdoor area and tap 'Get Location' again.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## SOS Message Delivery

### Copying the Message

Tapping **复制求助信息** writes the formatted SOS message to your clipboard using `navigator.clipboard.writeText`. On older browsers or iOS versions where the Clipboard API is unavailable, the page falls back to `document.execCommand('copy')` automatically. Either way, the full message is ready to paste into any app.

### Sending via SMS Deep-Link

Tapping **打开短信发送** opens the iPhone Messages app using the SMS URL scheme:

```
sms:<phone>&body=<encoded-message>
```

The `<phone>` field is pre-filled with your emergency contact's number, and `<encoded-message>` contains the fully URL-encoded SOS message. iOS requires you to tap **Send** yourself inside the Messages app — this is an iOS privacy requirement that cannot be bypassed.

## SOS Message Format {#sos-message-format}

Every message sent through PressureLight follows this exact format, giving your emergency contact everything they need to locate and assist you:

```plaintext
【PressureLight 紧急求助】
<Name> 触发了智能压力感知服装 SOS 报警。
当前位置：纬度 <lat>，经度 <lng>
定位精度：约 <X> 米
时间：<timestamp>
Apple 地图：https://maps.apple.com/?ll=<lat>,<lng>&q=PressureLight%20SOS
Google 地图：https://maps.google.com/?q=<lat>,<lng>
请尽快联系或前往协助。
```

- `<Name>` — the wearer name entered on the helper page
- `<lat>` and `<lng>` — latitude and longitude to **six decimal places** (approximately 0.1 m precision)
- `<X>` — GPS accuracy in meters reported by the device
- `<timestamp>` — the local date and time at the moment 'Get Location' was tapped
- The Apple Maps URL uses the format `https://maps.apple.com/?ll=<lat>,<lng>&q=PressureLight%20SOS`
- The Google Maps URL uses the format `https://maps.google.com/?q=<lat>,<lng>`

<Info>
  The SOS helper page **cannot silently access your location**. iPhone Safari enforces a mandatory user-permission prompt before any GPS data is read. You must tap 'Get Location' and grant location access for coordinates to appear. This is a deliberate privacy protection — your location is never captured in the background or without your action.
</Info>
