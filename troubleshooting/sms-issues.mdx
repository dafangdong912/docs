The PressureLight SOS page uses the iOS `sms:` URL scheme to open Apple Messages with a pre-filled emergency message. Because this mechanism relies on Safari and the native Messages app, a few things can go wrong. Work through the relevant section below to get your SOS message out quickly.

<Warning>
  The SOS page can never send a message without your manual confirmation — you must tap **Send** inside the Messages app yourself. This is a deliberate privacy protection; the page has no access to your messages or contacts.
</Warning>

## SMS Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="打开短信发送 does nothing">
    **Cause:** The `sms:` URL scheme is only supported in iPhone Safari. Chrome, Firefox, and other browsers — including in-app browsers inside WeChat, Instagram, or LINE — do not reliably handle this deep-link.

    **How to fix:**

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Confirm you are using iPhone Safari">
        Check the browser icon at the bottom of your screen. If it is not Safari, copy the SOS page URL and paste it into Safari's address bar.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Tap the button again">
        Tap **打开短信发送** in Safari. Safari hands the `sms:` link off to the Messages app automatically.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Confirm Messages is set up">
        If Safari still does nothing, verify that the Messages app is installed and configured on your device. Open **Messages** directly to confirm it works.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Note>
      The `sms:` deep-link is only reliably supported in **iPhone Safari**. Always open the PressureLight SOS page in Safari for reliable operation.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="SMS opens but has no phone number">
    **Cause:** The **紧急联系人手机号** (emergency contact phone number) field was empty when you tapped the button. The `sms:` URL requires a destination number to pre-fill the **To:** field in Messages. If no number is provided, Messages opens a blank compose screen with no recipient.

    **How to fix:**

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Return to the SOS page">
        Go back to the SOS page in Safari.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enter the phone number">
        Type your emergency contact's phone number into the **紧急联系人手机号** field. For international contacts, include the country code (for example, `+1` for the United States or `+44` for the United Kingdom).
      </Step>
      <Step title="Tap the button again">
        Tap **打开短信发送** again. Messages will now open with the number pre-filled in the **To:** field.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Tip>
      If you are already in the Messages compose screen, you can still type or paste the number directly into the **To:** field and send manually.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="SMS opens but the body is empty or garbled">
    **Cause:** On some iOS versions, a very long pre-filled message body may not transfer correctly through the `sms:` URL scheme, resulting in an empty or partially filled message body.

    **How to fix:**

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Copy the SOS message">
        Return to the SOS page and tap **复制求助信息** to copy the full SOS message to your clipboard.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Open Messages manually">
        Open the Messages app and start a new message to your emergency contact.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Paste and send">
        Long-press the message body field, tap **Paste**, then tap **Send**.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Copy button (复制求助信息) does nothing or shows an error">
    **Important:** If you tap **复制求助信息** before capturing your GPS location, the page will show the alert **「请先获取当前位置。」** (Please get your current location first). You must tap **Get Location** and wait for coordinates to appear before the copy button will work.

    If you have already captured your location and the button still fails, the cause is likely a clipboard permission issue or an unsupported browser.

    **How the copy mechanism works:** The page first tries `navigator.clipboard.writeText()`. If that call fails, it automatically falls back to `document.execCommand('copy')`. If both methods fail, you need to copy the text manually.

    **How to fix:**

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Capture location first">
        Tap **Get Location** and confirm that your coordinates appear on the page before tapping the copy button.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Grant clipboard permission">
        When Safari asks for clipboard permission, tap **Allow**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Retry in Safari">
        If the button still fails, make sure you are in **iPhone Safari** — the Clipboard API is most reliable there.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Copy manually as a last resort">
        Long-press the message text displayed on the page, tap **Select All**, then tap **Copy**. Paste into Messages, WeChat, WhatsApp, or any other app.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I sent the SMS but my contact didn't receive it">
    **Possible causes:**
    - The phone number format is incorrect (missing country code for international numbers).
    - You have no SMS signal at your current location.
    - Your contact's phone is off or out of range.

    **How to fix:**

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Check the phone number">
        Verify the number format. For international contacts, include the country code — for example, `+86` for China, `+1` for the United States, `+44` for the United Kingdom.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Check your signal">
        Look at your signal bars. If you have no cellular signal, copy the SOS message now and send it as soon as you regain connectivity.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Use an alternative channel">
        Your copied message works in any app — see the section below for alternatives.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Alternative Ways to Send the SOS Message

If SMS does not work, tap **复制求助信息** to copy the message, then paste and send it through any of the following:

- **WeChat** — open a chat with your emergency contact and paste
- **WhatsApp** — create a new message and paste
- **Email** — compose a new email and paste into the body
- **AirDrop** — paste into Notes and AirDrop the note to a nearby device

The copied message contains your GPS coordinates and map links, so it carries full location information regardless of how it is delivered.
