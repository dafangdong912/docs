---

When you tap **Get Location** on the PressureLight SOS page, your iPhone uses GPS to capture your coordinates and build an emergency map link. If location fails, the SOS message cannot include your position — so resolving these errors quickly matters.

<Note>
  The SOS page requires location permission. It cannot generate a map link without GPS coordinates.
</Note>

## Common GPS Errors

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Location permission denied (Error 1)">
    **Cause:** You previously tapped **Don't Allow** when Safari asked for location access, or Location Services for Safari is blocked in your iPhone Settings.

    **How to fix:**

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open Settings">
        Open the **Settings** app on your iPhone.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Navigate to Location Services">
        Go to **Privacy & Security** → **Location Services**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Find Safari Websites">
        Scroll down and tap **Safari Websites**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Set the permission">
        Select **While Using**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Retry">
        Return to the SOS page in Safari and tap **Get Location** again.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Tip>
      If you don't see Safari Websites in the list, make sure Location Services is toggled **on** at the top of the Location Services screen.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Position unavailable (Error 2)">
    **Cause:** Your device cannot determine your location. This typically happens indoors, in areas with poor GPS signal, or when Airplane Mode is enabled.

    **How to fix:**

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Move to a better location">
        Move near a window or, ideally, step outside.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Disable Airplane Mode if needed">
        If Airplane Mode is on, disable it: swipe into **Control Center** and tap the airplane icon to turn it off.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Wait for a satellite fix">
        Wait **5–10 seconds** for your iPhone to acquire a satellite signal.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Retry">
        Return to the SOS page and tap **Get Location** again.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Location timeout (Error 3)">
    **Cause:** GPS took longer than 15 seconds (15,000 ms) to get a fix. This often happens when you are inside a building, in a basement, or surrounded by tall structures that block satellite signals.

    **How to fix:**

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Move outdoors">
        Move to a more open area — outdoors is best.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Confirm Location Services is on">
        Confirm that **Location Services** is enabled in **Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Retry">
        Tap **Get Location** again to retry.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Tip>
      If you are already outside and still timing out, try locking and unlocking your iPhone to refresh the GPS radio, then retry.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Accuracy Is Very High (\>100 Meters)

This is not an error — the SOS page will still generate a map link — but a high accuracy value means your reported location is imprecise, which could make it harder for your emergency contact to find you.

**What to do:**

- Move outdoors and away from tall buildings or dense tree cover.
- Tap **Get Location** again to take a new reading. GPS accuracy improves as your iPhone establishes a stronger satellite lock.
- Check the accuracy figure included in the generated SOS message. If it still exceeds 100 meters, note this for your emergency contact so they know to search a wider area.

<Note>
  The SOS message includes the accuracy value in the message body so your emergency contact always knows how precise the location is. Outdoors, expect 5–30 m accuracy; indoors, accuracy can degrade to 100 m or more.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Test location capture before an emergency so you know it works on your device. Open the SOS page in iPhone Safari when you're outdoors and tap **Get Location** — confirm coordinates appear and the map link opens correctly.
</Tip>
