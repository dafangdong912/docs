title: "Understanding Map Links in PressureLight SOS Messages"

---

Every SOS message generated by PressureLight automatically includes two ready-to-tap map links — one for Apple Maps and one for Google Maps — built directly from the wearer's captured GPS coordinates. Your emergency contact does not need to type anything; tapping a link opens navigation immediately.

## The Two Map Links

### Apple Maps

```plaintext
https://maps.apple.com/?ll=<latitude>,<longitude>&q=PressureLight%20SOS
```

Tapping this link opens Apple Maps centred precisely on the wearer's captured coordinates. A pin labelled **PressureLight SOS** marks the location, making it immediately identifiable on the map. From there, your contact can start turn-by-turn walking or driving directions with a single tap.

### Google Maps

```plaintext
https://maps.google.com/?q=<latitude>,<longitude>
```

Tapping this link opens Google Maps — or the Google Maps website in a browser — with a pin placed at the wearer's coordinates. This link works on Android devices, desktop browsers, and any platform where Google Maps is preferred over Apple Maps.

## The '打开地图定位' Button

The SOS helper page itself also includes a **打开地图定位** button. Tapping it opens Apple Maps in a new tab directly from the page, showing the same coordinates captured during the GPS reading. Use this button to verify the pin location before sending your SOS message, or to share the map view from your own device.

<Note>
  Both map links point to a **static position** captured at the moment the GPS reading was taken. If the wearer has moved since then, the links will show the old location. If the situation is ongoing and the wearer's position may have changed, ask them to tap **获取我的当前位置** again on the SOS page to generate a fresh reading, then resend the updated message.
</Note>

## How Emergency Contacts Use the Links

When your contact receives the SOS message, here is what they should do:

- **Tap the Apple Maps link** to open turn-by-turn navigation directly on iPhone or Mac. Apple Maps will route them from their current location to the wearer's pin.
- **Tap the Google Maps link** if they are on Android, using a Windows PC, or simply prefer Google Maps. The link works in any browser.
- **Share the link with rescue services** — copy the Apple Maps or Google Maps URL from the message and send it to emergency services, a rescue coordinator, or a second contact who can assist.

<Tip>
  The accuracy value in the message tells your contact how precise the location is. If accuracy is greater than **50 metres**, ask the wearer to move to a more open area — away from buildings, underground car parks, or dense tree cover — and tap **获取我的当前位置** again to obtain a tighter fix before resending.
</Tip>

## Coordinate Format Reference

| Field | Example | Description |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| Latitude | 39.928700 | 6 decimal places; positive values are north of the equator |
| Longitude | 116.388200 | 6 decimal places; positive values are east of the prime meridian |
| Accuracy | 约 15 米 | Radius in metres within which the true position lies; lower is better |

Six decimal places of latitude or longitude corresponds to a precision of approximately **0.11 metres** — well within the tolerance required for pedestrian navigation. The accuracy figure reflects real-world signal conditions, not the coordinate precision itself.
