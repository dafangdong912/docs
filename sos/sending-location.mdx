The moment PressureLight's SOS alarm activates, your next priority is getting your exact location to someone who can help. The PressureLight SOS helper page runs directly in iPhone Safari — no app required — and generates a complete, ready-to-send emergency message with your GPS coordinates and map links in seconds.

## Sending Your Location Step by Step

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the SOS page in iPhone Safari">
    Navigate to the PressureLight SOS helper URL in Safari on your iPhone. The page loads instantly without any login or installation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your emergency contact's phone number">
    Type your contact's phone number into the **紧急联系人手机号** field. Include the country code if sending internationally (e.g. +86 for mainland China).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the wearer's name">
    Type the wearer's name into the **穿戴者姓名** field. If you leave this blank, the message defaults to **PressureLight 用户**. Using a real name helps your contact identify the situation immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap '获取我的当前位置'">
    Tap the **获取我的当前位置** button. If Safari prompts you to allow location access, tap **Allow**. The page requests your position using high-accuracy GPS (15-second timeout, no cached results), so obtaining a fix may take a few seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for coordinates to appear">
    Once the GPS reading is complete, the page displays:
    - **Latitude** — 6 decimal places
    - **Longitude** — 6 decimal places
    - **Accuracy** — in metres (lower is better)
    - **Timestamp** — date and time of the reading
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the generated SOS message">
    The page automatically populates the message text area with a fully formatted SOS message using your coordinates and the name you entered. Read it over to confirm the details look correct.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the message">
    Choose your sending method:

    **Option A — Copy and paste:**
    Tap **复制求助信息** to copy the entire message to your clipboard. Open any messaging app — WeChat, WhatsApp, iMessage, email — paste the message, and send it.

    **Option B — Open iPhone Messages directly:**
    Tap **打开短信发送**. iPhone Messages opens with the recipient pre-filled and the SOS message already in the body. Tap **Send** to dispatch it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## SOS Message Format

Every message generated by the SOS helper page follows this exact format. The example below uses sample coordinates for illustration:

```plaintext
【PressureLight 紧急求助】
张明 触发了智能压力感知服装 SOS 报警。
当前位置：纬度 39.928700，经度 116.388200
定位精度：约 15 米
时间：2024/06/12 14:35:22
Apple 地图：https://maps.apple.com/?ll=39.928700,116.388200&q=PressureLight%20SOS
Google 地图：https://maps.google.com/?q=39.928700,116.388200
请尽快联系或前往协助。
```

The two map links at the bottom let your contact open turn-by-turn navigation immediately, on either Apple Maps or Google Maps, without needing to type in any coordinates manually.

<Info>
  Tapping **打开短信发送** opens iPhone Messages with the message pre-filled, but it does **not** send automatically. You must tap **Send** yourself. This is an intentional privacy safeguard — the page cannot dispatch SMS messages on your behalf without your explicit confirmation.
</Info>

<Tip>
  If the **紧急联系人手机号** field is empty when you tap **打开短信发送**, iPhone Messages will open a new SMS with no recipient pre-filled. Always enter the phone number first so your message reaches the right person without delay.
</Tip>

## Sharing via Other Apps

After tapping **复制求助信息**, the full SOS message is on your clipboard and you can paste it anywhere:

- **WeChat** — open a chat, long-press the input field, and tap Paste.
- **WhatsApp** — open a conversation, tap the message field, and paste.
- **Phone call notes** — paste the coordinates into a note so you can read them aloud to emergency services.
- **Email** — paste into the body of an email if SMS is unavailable.

The message text is plain Unicode and renders correctly in every major messaging platform.
