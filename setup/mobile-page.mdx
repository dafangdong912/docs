The PressureLight SOS helper page is your command center when the garment triggers an alert. It runs entirely in iPhone Safari — no app download, no account, no setup fees. Spend two minutes familiarizing yourself with it now so operating it under stress is second nature.

<Warning>
  Always open the SOS page in **iPhone Safari**. Other browsers such as Chrome or Firefox may not support the `sms:` deep-link format and can provide lower GPS accuracy. The page is designed and tested exclusively for Mobile Safari on iOS.
</Warning>

## Open the Page

You have two options:

- **Scan the QR code** printed on the tag inside your PressureLight garment. Your iPhone camera opens the link directly in Safari.
- **Type the URL** printed on the same tag directly into the Safari address bar.

Both methods take you to the same helper page. Verify you see the blinking red status indicator before continuing.

## Add the Page to Your Home Screen

Saving the SOS page as a Home Screen shortcut lets you open it instantly — the same way you would launch any app.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the page in Safari">
    Navigate to the SOS helper page URL using the QR code or by typing the address into Safari.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap the Share button">
    Tap the **Share** button at the bottom of the Safari toolbar — it looks like a box with an arrow pointing upward.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select 'Add to Home Screen'">
    Scroll down the share sheet and tap **Add to Home Screen**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name and confirm">
    Enter the name **PressureLight SOS** so you can find it instantly, then tap **Add**. The icon appears on your Home Screen.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Page Anatomy

Each section of the helper page has a specific role. Learn them before you need them.

| Element | What It Does |
|---|---|
| **Status indicator** (blinking red dot) | Shows the page is active and ready. A steady or absent dot may indicate a script error — reload the page. |
| **Get Location button** (获取我的当前位置) | Requests your current GPS position from iOS. Tap this first after opening the page. |
| **Emergency contact number field** (紧急联系人手机号) | Accepts the mobile number of your emergency contact. This pre-fills the SMS deep-link recipient. |
| **Wearer name field** | Your name as it will appear in the SOS message so your contact knows exactly who sent the alert. |
| **Coordinates display** | Shows your latitude, longitude, accuracy in meters, and the timestamp of the last GPS fix. |
| **SOS message textarea** | The full pre-formatted message that will be sent — includes your name, coordinates, accuracy, timestamp, and links to Apple Maps and Google Maps. |
| **Copy button** (复制求助信息) | Copies the complete SOS message to your clipboard so you can paste it into any app. |
| **SMS button** (打开短信发送) | Opens the iPhone Messages app with the contact number and SOS message pre-filled. You still tap Send to dispatch it. |
| **Open Map button** | Opens your coordinates in Apple Maps so you or a responder can confirm the location visually. |

<Note>
  The page does not store your data anywhere. Your name, contact number, and GPS coordinates exist only in the current browser session. Closing the tab clears everything.
</Note>
